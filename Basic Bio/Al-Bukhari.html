Al-Bukhari studied Hadeeth at an early age and travelled widely over the Muslim world collecting Hadeeth and compiled the most authetic ones in his book Al-Jami'As-Sahih, which later became Sahih Al-Bukhari and contains 2602 Hadeeth which he selected from the thousands of Hadeeth that he had memorized. Al-Bukhari studied under famous Hadeeth Imam like Imams like Malik bin Anas and Yahya bin Ma'im. He studied in Samarqaed, the present day capital of Uzbekistan. Nowadays, the capital is Tashkent, the night of Eid-ul-Fitr, 256AH. Al-Bukhari was unique in the science of methodology of Hadeeth and his book SAl-Jami'As-Sahih is considered to be the most authentic book after the Qur'an. Saheeh Bukhari has been translated to English and is easily available.
